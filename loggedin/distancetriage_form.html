Distance Counselling: Informed Consent










In preparation for your distance counselling appointment, please ensure that you have a private and quiet space available to you. If you receiving counselling over the phone, please ensure that no one else has access to that phone line. If you are seeking online counselling, please ensure the online counselling platform, ZOOM, is installed and that your device is fully charged. We are unable to permit extra time for technology set-up and power outages during the counselling appointment. Please ensure that you have read all and familiarized yourself with the below information prior to your first online counselling session.

Section A
Information about Online Counselling
A minimum of 24 hours notice is required for cancelling or changing online appointments that have been pre-booked. If you booked same-day, and need to cancel, please call to let us know. Appointments can be cancelling or rescheduled by contacting 613-533-2506.

Distance counselling is not an appropriate forum for providing crisis interventions. If you are experiencing a mental health emergency such as suicidal or homicidal ideation, please visit your nearest emergency department. A referral to a provider within your geographical location is highly recommended in the above situations.

  • The counsellor will be asking you to state your exact location prior to the beginning of the session. This is to maximize our opportunity to get help to you in the case of an emergency.
  • While distance counselling has been found to be effective, please be advised that the potential for miscommunications are greater than when being counselled in person.
  • Please be advised that given provincial regulations for regulated health care professionals, we are unable to provide distance counselling outside of the province of Ontario at this time. Most Regulated health care professionals must be licensed in the province or country where the client is physically located in order to provide distance counselling.

Section B
Confidentiality and Technology
All information disclosed within sessions is confidential and will not be revealed to anyone ourside of Student Wellness Services without your written permission with some exceptions.
Please be advised that while we endeavor to uphold the highest standards of privacy as it pertains to your personal information and in compliance with PHIPA (Personal Health Information and Privacy Act), we are unable to guarantee that your information will not be subjected to security breaches when using an online communication platform.

Further you are signing that you have read and understoof the following:
  • Email is not a secure forum for communication. Please keep ema il communication restricted to content related to scheduling appointments and do not release personal information in the email. We are able to send you documents securely via OneDrive and we suggest you use that platform to send us back any paperwork.
  • Email will be retained either by paper and/or electronic copies, as they will become part of your file.
  • Upon completing the online counselling session, please ensure that you sign out through the logout process or end session.

By signing this document, you are providing your consent and acknowledgement that you have read and understood the limitations of distance counselling, the limits to confidentiality, and are agreeing to abide by the conditions set forth in this document.
